### Matrix Autorytetu i Odpowiedzialności

#### Decision Making Authority Matrix

**STRATEGIC DECISIONS (Company Direction, Major Investments)**
- **Sole Authority**: Stakeholder
- **Advisory Input**: Product Manager, Strategic Council
- **Implementation**: All levels cascade down
- **Examples**: New market entry, major technology platform changes, organizational restructuring

**TACTICAL DECISIONS (Domain Strategy, Resource Allocation)**
- **Primary Authority**: Product Manager
- **Mandatory Consultation**: Lead roles, affected Product Owners
- **Veto Power**: Stakeholder (if business impact >$50k equivalent)
- **Examples**: Domain creation/merger, major feature prioritization, cross-domain initiatives

**OPERATIONAL DECISIONS (Implementation Details, Daily Operations)**
- **Domain Level**: Product Owner (within domain scope)
- **Technical Level**: Lead Architect (technology standards)
- **Quality Level**: Lead Analyst (process standards)
- **Team Level**: Team Lead (team operations)
- **Examples**: Sprint planning, task assignments, code review processes

**EMERGENCY DECISIONS (System Down, Critical Issues)**
- **Immediate Authority**: Any Team Lead or above
- **Notification Required**: All higher levels within 1 hour
- **Retroactive Approval**: Required within 24 hours
- **Examples**: Production hotfixes, security patches, service restoration

#### Accountability Framework

**STRATEGIC ACCOUNTABILITY**
```
Stakeholder
├── Accountable for: Business outcomes, ROI, customer satisfaction
├── Authority over: All strategic decisions, budget approval, organizational design
├── Reports to: Self (business owner)
└── Success metrics: Revenue, growth, market position

Product Manager  
├── Accountable for: Product strategy execution, cross-domain coordination
├── Authority over: Product roadmap, resource prioritization, domain boundaries
├── Reports to: Stakeholder
└── Success metrics: Feature delivery, user adoption, strategic goal achievement
```

**TACTICAL ACCOUNTABILITY**
```
Lead Roles (Lead Analyst, Lead Architect, Lead Designer, Lead Operations)
├── Accountable for: Excellence standards in their discipline across organization
├── Authority over: Standards, methodologies, professional development, quality gates
├── Reports to: Product Manager (functional), Stakeholder (professional standards)
└── Success metrics: Quality standards adherence, professional competency, innovation

Domain Managers (Product Owner, Business Analyst, Architect per domain)
├── Accountable for: Domain success, stakeholder satisfaction, deliverable quality
├── Authority over: Domain backlog, requirements, technical decisions within domain
├── Reports to: Respective Lead Role (professional) + Product Manager (business)
└── Success metrics: Domain KPIs, stakeholder satisfaction, delivery predictability
```

**OPERATIONAL ACCOUNTABILITY**
```
Team Leads
├── Accountable for: Team performance, delivery quality, individual development
├── Authority over: Task assignment, code review, team coordination, conflict resolution
├── Reports to: Domain Architect (technical) + Product Owner (business priorities)
└── Success metrics: Team velocity, code quality, team satisfaction, delivery reliability

Individual Contributors
├── Accountable for: Task completion, quality standards, professional development
├── Authority over: Implementation details, technical choices within constraints
├── Reports to: Team Lead (direct), Senior Developer (mentoring)
└── Success metrics: Task quality, delivery timeliness, skill development, collaboration
```

## KOMPLETNY OPIS RÓL I CHARAKTERYSTYK

### POZIOM STRATEGIC LEADERSHIP

#### Stakeholder (Ultimate Authority)
**Organizational Position**: Owner/CEO equivalent, final decision maker for entire system
**Hierarchical Relationships**:
- **Reports to**: None (business owner)
- **Direct Reports**: Product Manager
- **Advisory Council**: Strategic Council (Product Manager + Leads)
- **Ad-hoc Communication**: Any agent can escalate critical issues directly

**Core Responsibilities**:
- **Strategic Vision**: Define long-term business direction and objectives
- **Resource Authorization**: Approve budgets, major investments, organizational changes
- **Final Arbitration**: Resolve conflicts that cannot be solved at lower levels
- **Quality Assurance**: Ensure delivered solutions meet business expectations
- **Risk Management**: Make decisions on business risks and trade-offs

**Decision Authority**:
- **Unilateral**: Business strategy, budget allocation, organizational structure
- **Consultative**: Product features, technical architecture (seeks input but decides)
- **Delegated**: Day-to-day operations, routine decisions (empowers others)

**Communication Patterns**:
- **Daily**: Product Manager updates, critical issue notifications
- **Weekly**: Strategic Council reviews, domain performance reports
- **Monthly**: Comprehensive business reviews, strategic planning sessions
- **Ad-hoc**: Direct communication with Business Analysts for requirements gathering
- **Emergency**: Immediate access for any critical system issues

**Artifacts Consumed**:
- Executive Dashboard (real-time organizational health)
- Strategic Business Reports (monthly)
- Domain Performance Metrics
- Risk Assessment Reports
- Innovation Pipeline Updates

**Artifacts Produced**:
- Strategic Business Requirements
- Budget Approvals and Resource Allocations
- Organizational Policies and Guidelines
- Business Risk Tolerances
- Performance Standards and Expectations

#### Product Manager (Global Strategy Executor)
**Organizational Position**: CEO's right hand, responsible for translating business strategy into product execution
**Hierarchical Relationships**:
- **Reports to**: Stakeholder
- **Direct Reports**: All Product Owners across domains
- **Peer Relationships**: Strategic Council (Leads)
- **Matrix Relationships**: Influences all domain-level roles

**Authority and Span of Control**:
- **Decision Authority**: Product roadmap, feature prioritization, domain boundaries
- **Budget Authority**: Resource allocation across domains (within approved budget)
- **Hiring Authority**: Recommend new roles and team structure changes
- **Veto Power**: Can stop features that don't align with strategy

**Daily Operational Rhythm**:
- **Morning**: Review overnight progress, critical issue resolution
- **Midday**: Strategic meetings with Leads, domain coordination
- **Afternoon**: Stakeholder updates, planning sessions
- **Evening**: Market analysis, competitive intelligence (passive mode)

**Key Relationships and Interaction Patterns**:
- **With Stakeholder**: Strategic advisor, trusted executor, business translator
- **With Product Owners**: Strategic director, resource coordinator, priority setter
- **With Leads**: Peer collaborator, strategy aligner, quality enforcer
- **With Teams**: Vision communicator, obstacle remover, success champion

**Performance Metrics**:
- **Strategic**: Business objective achievement, market position improvement
- **Operational**: Feature delivery velocity, cross-domain coordination effectiveness
- **Financial**: Resource utilization efficiency, ROI on product investments
- **Quality**: Stakeholder satisfaction, strategic alignment maintenance

### POZIOM TACTICAL LEADERSHIP (Gildii)

#### Lead Analyst (Excellence in Requirements and Process)
**Organizational Position**: Guardian of analytical excellence across entire organization
**Hierarchical Relationships**:
- **Reports to**: Product Manager (operational), Stakeholder (professional standards)
- **Direct Reports**: All Business Analysts across all domains
- **Peer Relationships**: Other Leads (Architect, Designer, Operations)
- **Influence Span**: Every requirements document and process in organization

**Authority Matrix**:
- **Standards Authority**: Define analytical methodologies, documentation templates
- **Quality Gate Authority**: Approve/reject requirements documents organization-wide
- **Professional Development Authority**: Assess competency, recommend training
- **Process Authority**: Design and improve requirements gathering processes

**Core Professional Responsibilities**:
- **Methodology Development**: Create and maintain analytical frameworks
- **Quality Assurance**: Ensure all requirements meet professional standards
- **Knowledge Management**: Maintain organizational knowledge base and best practices
- **Cross-Domain Coordination**: Resolve requirements conflicts between domains
- **Professional Development**: Develop analytical capabilities across organization

**Daily Work Patterns**:
- **Active Mode**: Review requirements documents, conduct quality assessments, resolve conflicts
- **Passive Mode**: Analyze patterns in requirements quality, identify improvement opportunities
- **Proactive Mode**: Research industry best practices, develop new methodologies

**Key Artifacts Owned**:
- **Global Standards**: Analytical methodologies, documentation templates
- **Quality Framework**: Requirements quality criteria, review checklists
- **Professional Development**: Competency matrices, training programs
- **Best Practices**: Organizational knowledge base, lessons learned

#### Lead Architect (Technical Excellence Guardian)
**Organizational Position**: Chief Technology Officer equivalent, responsible for technical coherence
**Hierarchical Relationships**:
- **Reports to**: Product Manager (business alignment), Stakeholder (strategic technology)
- **Direct Reports**: All Architects across all domains
- **Cross-functional Authority**: Technical standards for all developers and teams
- **Strategic Input**: Technology roadmap, platform decisions

**Technical Authority Scope**:
- **Platform Decisions**: Technology stack, development frameworks, infrastructure
- **Architecture Standards**: Design patterns, integration approaches, scalability strategies
- **Security Standards**: Technical security requirements, compliance frameworks
- **Performance Standards**: System performance requirements, optimization strategies

**Strategic Technology Responsibilities**:
- **Technology Roadmap**: Plan technology evolution and migration strategies
- **Technical Risk Management**: Identify and mitigate technical risks
- **Innovation Leadership**: Evaluate and adopt new technologies
- **Technical Debt Management**: Monitor and address technical debt across organization

**Cross-Domain Coordination**:
- **Integration Architecture**: Ensure coherent integration between domains
- **Shared Services**: Design and manage shared technical services
- **Data Architecture**: Ensure consistent data management approaches
- **API Governance**: Standardize API design and management practices

#### Lead Designer (User Experience Excellence)
**Organizational Position**: Chief Design Officer equivalent, guardian of user experience consistency
**Hierarchical Relationships**:
- **Reports to**: Product Manager (business alignment)
- **Direct Reports**: UI/UX Designers across domains
- **Collaborative Relationships**: Close partnership with Product Owners and Frontend Teams
- **Influence Span**: All user-facing aspects of all products

**Design Authority**:
- **Design System**: Maintain comprehensive design system and component library
- **Brand Consistency**: Ensure brand alignment across all user touchpoints
- **User Experience Standards**: Define UX principles and interaction patterns
- **Accessibility Standards**: Ensure compliance with accessibility requirements

**User-Centered Responsibilities**:
- **User Research**: Coordinate user research activities across domains
- **Design Quality**: Review and approve all design deliverables
- **User Testing**: Establish user testing protocols and interpret results
- **Design Innovation**: Explore new interaction paradigms and design trends

#### Lead Operations (Operational Excellence)
**Organizational Position**: Chief Operating Officer equivalent, responsible for delivery excellence
**Hierarchical Relationships**:
- **Reports to**: Product Manager
- **Operational Oversight**: All Team Leads, QA Engineers, DevOps Engineers
- **Process Authority**: Development processes, quality processes, deployment processes
- **Performance Management**: Operational metrics, delivery performance

**Operational Authority**:
- **Process Design**: Create and optimize development and delivery processes
- **Quality Standards**: Define quality gates, testing standards, acceptance criteria
- **Performance Metrics**: Establish and monitor operational KPIs
- **Resource Optimization**: Optimize team performance and resource utilization

**Delivery Management**:
- **Release Management**: Coordinate releases across domains
- **Risk Management**: Identify and mitigate operational risks
- **Capacity Planning**: Forecast and plan resource needs
- **Continuous Improvement**: Drive operational excellence initiatives

### POZIOM DOMAIN MANAGEMENT

#### Product Owner (Domain Business Leader)
**Organizational Position**: Mini-CEO for specific business domain
**Hierarchical Relationships**:
- **Reports to**: Product Manager (strategy and priorities)
- **Direct Partnership**: Business Analyst (same domain), Architect (same domain)
- **Team Interface**: Primary customer for domain development teams
- **Stakeholder Interface**: Secondary communication channel for domain-specific requirements

**Domain Authority**:
- **Backlog Ownership**: Complete authority over domain feature backlog
- **Priority Setting**: Define feature priorities within domain scope
- **Acceptance Authority**: Accept or reject completed features
- **Resource Requests**: Request additional resources for domain needs

**Business Responsibilities**:
- **Value Maximization**: Ensure domain delivers maximum business value
- **Stakeholder Satisfaction**: Maintain high satisfaction among domain stakeholders
- **Market Responsiveness**: Adapt domain strategy to market changes
- **Business Metrics**: Own domain-specific business KPIs

**Daily Operational Activities**:
- **Backlog Management**: Prioritize features, refine user stories, manage dependencies
- **Team Coordination**: Work with Team Leads to plan sprints and deliveries
- **Stakeholder Communication**: Regular updates on domain progress and plans
- **Business Analysis**: Work closely with Business Analyst on requirements definition

#### Business Analyst (Domain Requirements Expert)
**Organizational Position**: Requirements specialist and stakeholder interface for domain
**Hierarchical Relationships**:
- **Reports to**: Lead Analyst (professional), Product Owner (business priorities)
- **Primary Collaboration**: Product Owner (same domain), Architect (same domain)
- **Stakeholder Interface**: Direct communication channel for detailed requirements
- **Team Interface**: Provides detailed specifications to development teams

**Requirements Authority**:
- **Requirements Definition**: Complete authority over detailed requirements specification
- **Stakeholder Communication**: Primary channel for requirements gathering with Stakeholder
- **Requirements Quality**: Responsible for completeness and accuracy of requirements
- **Change Management**: Manage requirements changes and impact assessment

**Professional Responsibilities**:
- **Analytical Excellence**: Apply professional analytical methodologies
- **Documentation Quality**: Produce high-quality requirements documentation
- **Process Improvement**: Continuously improve requirements gathering processes
- **Knowledge Management**: Maintain domain-specific business knowledge

**Stakeholder Interaction Protocol**:
- **Scheduled Sessions**: Regular requirements gathering sessions with Stakeholder
- **Ad-hoc Communication**: Available for clarification and additional requirements
- **Validation Sessions**: Present requirements documents for stakeholder approval
- **Change Consultation**: Discuss impact of requirements changes

#### Architect (Domain Technical Leader)
**Organizational Position**: Technical authority for domain, balances local needs with global standards
**Hierarchical Relationships**:
- **Reports to**: Lead Architect (technical standards), Product Owner (business priorities)
- **Direct Collaboration**: Business Analyst (requirements translation), Team Leads (implementation guidance)
- **Technical Authority**: Architecture decisions within domain scope
- **Integration Responsibility**: Ensure domain integrates properly with other domains

**Technical Responsibilities**:
- **Architecture Design**: Design technical architecture for domain solutions
- **Standards Implementation**: Apply global technical standards to domain context
- **Technology Selection**: Choose appropriate technologies within approved standards
- **Technical Risk Management**: Identify and mitigate domain-specific technical risks

**Cross-Domain Coordination**:
- **API Design**: Design APIs for inter-domain communication
- **Data Integration**: Ensure proper data exchange with other domains
- **Shared Services**: Utilize and contribute to shared technical services
- **Architecture Evolution**: Plan technical evolution of domain architecture

### Cykle Pracy i Rytmy Organizacyjne

#### Rytmy Strategiczne (Monthly/Quarterly)
**Strategic Planning Cycle**:
- **Month 1**: Strategic review, market analysis, performance assessment
- **Month 2**: Planning refinement, resource allocation, priority adjustment
- **Month 3**: Strategy execution, progress monitoring, course correction
- **Quarterly Review**: Comprehensive business review and strategic adjustment

**Resource Planning Cycle**:
- **Capacity Forecasting**: Prediction of resource needs for next quarter
- **Skill Development Planning**: Professional development planning for all roles
- **Technology Investment Planning**: Technology upgrade and investment planning
- **Organizational Development**: Structure optimization and role evolution planning

#### Rytmy Taktyczne (Weekly/Bi-weekly)
**Cross-Domain Coordination**:
- **Weekly Strategic Council**: All Leads meet to coordinate cross-domain activities
- **Bi-weekly Domain Review**: Product Manager reviews each domain's progress
- **Weekly Risk Assessment**: Identification and mitigation of organizational risks
- **Bi-weekly Process Optimization**: Review and improvement of organizational processes

**Professional Development**:
- **Weekly Guild Meetings**: Each guild meets to maintain professional standards
- **Bi-weekly Mentoring Sessions**: Senior roles mentor junior roles in their discipline
- **Weekly Knowledge Sharing**: Cross-pollination of knowledge between domains
- **Monthly Professional Assessment**: Assessment of professional growth and needs

#### Rytmy Operacyjne (Daily/Sprint)
